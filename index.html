Reflection on the Group Work

Developing a virtual exhibition on Fujian-Taiwan culture

Guang Yang

This reflection outlines our group's journey in building a 3D virtual exhibition centred on Fujian-Taiwan culture. It discusses our rationale for selecting the topic and the Metasteps platform, evaluates the success of our implementation, and critically analyses the technical limitations we encountered regarding data sovereignty and open access.

View our virtual exhibition here: Open Virtual Exhibition

Topic and Platform Selection

We decided to build a 3D virtual exhibition on Fujian-Taiwan culture, because this topic aligned closely with our group’s interests. In particular, I am personally interested in the language issues in the region from a linguistic perspective, especially the Hokkien language. We chose to create the exhibition using Metasteps, because:

  1. Suitability of Materials: The materials we collected were well suited to an exhibition format.
  2. Technical Sophistication: Metasteps offers a sophisticated, high-tech 3D environment that we wanted to make use of, benefiting not only this project, but also our future study. The virtual exhibition hall on Metasteps supports a wide range of contents, such as text, images, videos, and 3D objects, which was very promising and intriguing for our project.

Implementation and Critical Review

Our implementation worked well. The final exhibition met our initial goals. I think everything worked well. However, if I were to do it again, I would prefer choosing a more “open” platform. Metasteps confines our work to its own ecosystem: the exhibition cannot be exported, embedded elsewhere, or shared in a standalone format. It would be better if we could export our virtual exhibition in some kind of file format (for 3D virtual exhibitions) or embed it to a webpage if needed. Or, even better if the source code of the entire platform could be open source.
